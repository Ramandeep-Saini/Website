Nice To Meet You

My Name is Ramandeep Saini

Hey there! I’m a 16-year-old from Brampton, Ontario who's very passionate about technology. I love writing about technology for online publications, such as the Data Driven Investor.

I am continuously improving my coding skills, and have been recognized by companies such as Microsoft for my work. For last year’s Microsoft Discover AI challenge, my team and I built a neural network to prevent outbreaks of Malaria. We developed this into a startup (the company is called Prohibio Health), and the one-pager can be found here, while an article about the startup can be found here.

Furthermore, a recent project I have been working on is called Cryptic Languages. This is a website I created that features coding tutorials to encourage kids, especially girls, to learn to code. Check it out here.

Another passion I developed in the last couple of months is researching longevity. In my portfolio, I've included a science report on how senescent cells effect the aging process, and presented a literature review I wrote regarding senescent cells at the Young Scientists' Journal Conference that was held at the University of Cambridge. That's a wrap on me, thanks for your interest in my work!

Current Projects

Prohibio Health

Last year, I was developing an AI project called Prohibio Health with three other teammates. The focus of the company is to prevent outbreaks of insect-borne diseases, like Malaria, by simulating the disease transmission factors (e.g. insect breeding and migration).

Cryptic Languages

Cryptic Languages is an organization I founded to encourage students, especially girls to learn to code through tutorials and webinars. To date, I have impacted over 170 students from 19 countries, and partnered with my local library to host a webinar for their city-wide hackathon. For my work, I was a finalist for the Aspiring Teen Award, presented by the Women in Tech Global Awards. Check out the Cryptic Languages website here.

Art Blog

My art blog is a project that I run year round, and I try to post weekly on it. It's a platform for me to share my thoughts and opinions on various global issues, such as climate change and misogyny.

Reducing Online Harassment

As a part of the Girls Belong Here Program run by Plan International Canada, I am currently working with Twitter to reduce online harassment on their platform. I presented my recommendations to reduce online abuse to the Head of Public Policy at Twitter Canada, Michele Austin, who has invited me to present my reccomendations to a global public policy team and at the Twitter for Good Day company-wide event.

Harmony

I'm currently working on developing an app called Harmony to combat the mental health impacts caused by the pandemic for this year's Technovation Challenge. Last year, I developed an app called EcoPal to track carbon emissions, which you can find below.

Volunteering

My volunteer roles include volunteering at the Tall Pines Long Term Care Centre and being a Teen Ambassador for the Canadian Association of Girls in Science (CAGIS). My usual duties at Tall Pines include volunteering with the art therapy program to help residents with motor disabilities complete artwork, and to engage with the residents. As a Teen Ambassador for CAGIS, I advise the CAGIS national team on how to improve future events, participate in CAGIS' virtual events, and plan events to spark an interest in STEM in young girls.
